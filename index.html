01 — Overview

About the Project

ReliefVision is an intelligent disaster analysis platform designed to bridge the gap between satellite data and on-ground emergency response. It ingests pre- and post-disaster satellite imagery, applies the Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM) to detect pixel-level structural changes, and translates raw damage signals into actionable, prioritized alerts.

The system is built to serve disaster management authorities, NGOs, and first responders who require rapid situational awareness in the critical hours following a catastrophic event — when every minute of delayed information translates directly into preventable loss of life and property.

02 — The Problem

Problem Statement

When disasters strike — earthquakes, floods, cyclones — emergency teams are overwhelmed with the sheer scale of damage assessment. Traditional manual analysis of aerial or satellite imagery is painfully slow, highly subjective, and prone to human error under pressure.

Hours are lost before decisions are even made, while affected communities wait without aid. This bottleneck results in misallocated resources, delayed rescue operations, and a higher preventable toll on human lives.

Need for a System

The global frequency of climate-related disasters has surged by over 83% in the past two decades. Existing tools are either too slow, too expensive, or require specialized GIS expertise most field teams simply do not have.

There is an urgent need for a lightweight, automated system that can process satellite imagery rapidly, produce structured damage assessments, and communicate findings in a format that non-technical decision-makers can act upon immediately — without any specialized training.

03 — Our Approach

Proposed Solution

ReliefVision uses the Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM) — a perceptual metric that quantifies differences in luminance, contrast, and structure between two images — to compare pre- and post-disaster satellite imagery at the pixel level. This produces a detailed change-detection map that reveals exactly where and how severely infrastructure has been affected.

The system then applies adaptive thresholding to classify detected changes into four damage tiers: Minimal, Moderate, Severe, and Critical. Each tier is assigned a dynamic priority score, and the system automatically generates structured alert packets containing the damage coordinates, severity classification, estimated impact radius, and recommended response actions.

The entire pipeline runs without manual intervention and is accessible through an interactive Streamlit web application, enabling deployment without any local infrastructure, GIS software, or specialized hardware.

04 — How It Works

Methodology

🛰️
Image Acquisition
Pre- and post-disaster satellite images sourced from open APIs or uploaded manually for processing.
⚙️
Preprocessing
Images are resized, normalized, and co-registered to ensure precise spatial alignment before comparison.
☁️
Cloud Masking
Cloud-covered regions are detected and excluded to prevent false positives during structural change analysis.
📊
SSIM Computation
SSIM scores computed pixel-by-pixel to generate a high-resolution map of structural changes across the scene.
🎯
Thresholding
Adaptive thresholds classify SSIM difference scores into four distinct damage severity categories.
📋
Damage Estimation
Damaged zones quantified by area, severity, and impact score to produce actionable, readable field reports.

End-to-End Automation

The workflow is fully automated with zero manual intervention between stages. Once satellite images are uploaded, the system preprocesses and aligns them, runs SSIM comparison to generate a granular difference map, identifies changed zones above the damage threshold, classifies each zone's severity tier, assigns a response priority score, and outputs a structured alert packet — all within seconds. Results are displayed visually in the Streamlit interface for immediate interpretation by field teams.

06 — Output

Alert System

ReliefVision's alert engine translates raw damage data into structured, human-readable alert packets. Each alert contains the geographic zone ID, damage severity classification, priority level, estimated affected area, and a set of recommended response actions — enabling decision-makers to act with precision and speed rather than guesswork under pressure.

07 — Roadmap

Short-Term Goals

  • Integration with real-time satellite APIs (Sentinel Hub, Planet Labs) for live imagery feeds without manual upload
  • Incorporation of live weather data to adjust severity thresholds dynamically based on environmental conditions
  • Multi-disaster type support including flood extent mapping and wildfire perimeter detection
  • Exportable PDF alert reports for offline distribution to field teams without internet access

Long-Term Vision

  • Deep learning classification using CNNs and Vision Transformers for accuracy beyond what SSIM alone can achieve
  • Full GIS mapping integration with interactive geospatial visualization and zone-level drill-down capabilities
  • Mobile-first dashboard for field operatives with offline-capable alert access and GPS-guided navigation
  • Formal partnership with NDMA and UN-OCHA for deployment in active international disaster response operations
